5 Myths About Solar Energy

Published on April 25, 2025 by Re One Astralis

Introduction

Despite the growing popularity of solar energy, many misconceptions still prevent people from adopting this sustainable solution. These myths often stem from outdated information or a lack of understanding about modern solar technology. At Re One Astralis, we believe in empowering our clients in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, with accurate information. In this blog post, we’ll debunk five common myths about solar energy to help you make an informed decision about going solar.

Myth 1: Solar Energy Is Too Expensive

One of the most persistent myths is that solar energy is prohibitively expensive. While the upfront cost of solar panels was high in the past, prices have dropped significantly over the years. Additionally, government subsidies, tax incentives, and financing options in Gujarat, India, make solar more affordable than ever. Over time, the savings on electricity bills often outweigh the initial investment, making solar a cost-effective choice.

Myth 2: Solar Panels Don’t Work in Cloudy Weather

Many people believe that solar panels are ineffective in cloudy or rainy weather. While solar panels perform best in direct sunlight, they can still generate electricity on cloudy days. Modern panels are designed to capture diffused sunlight, producing energy even in less-than-ideal conditions. In Ahmedabad, where sunny days are abundant, solar panels remain a reliable energy source year-round.

Myth 3: Solar Panels Require High Maintenance

Another common myth is that solar panels require constant maintenance. In reality, solar panels are designed to be low-maintenance. They have no moving parts, reducing the risk of mechanical failure, and typically need only occasional cleaning to remove dust or debris. At Re One Astralis, we also offer maintenance services to ensure your system stays in top condition with minimal effort on your part.

Myth 4: Solar Energy Isn’t Reliable

Some believe that solar energy is unreliable due to its dependence on sunlight. However, advancements in energy storage technology, such as batteries, allow you to store excess energy for use at night or during outages. Additionally, hybrid systems can connect to the grid as a backup. Solar energy is a dependable source of power, especially in sunny regions like Gujarat, where Re One Astralis ensures reliable installations.

Myth 5: Solar Panels Decrease Property Value

Contrary to the myth that solar panels decrease property value, studies show that they often increase it. Homes with solar panels are attractive to buyers due to the promise of lower energy bills and environmental benefits. In a growing city, where sustainability is valued, solar-equipped properties can stand out in the real estate market, offering a significant return on investment.

Conclusion

By debunking these five myths—solar energy being too expensive, ineffective in cloudy weather, high-maintenance, unreliable, and detrimental to property value—we aim to clarify misconceptions and highlight the true benefits of solar power.
