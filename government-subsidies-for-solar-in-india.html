Government Subsidies for Solar in India 2025

Published on May 17, 2025 by Re One Astralis

Introduction

India is making significant strides toward a sustainable future by promoting renewable energy, particularly solar power. To encourage adoption, the Indian government offers various subsidies that make solar installations more affordable for homeowners, businesses, and farmers. These financial incentives are crucial in reducing the upfront costs of solar systems, helping households in cities like Ahmedabad, Gujarat, transition to clean energy. In this blog, Re One Astralis explores the key government subsidies for solar energy in India in 2025, how to apply for them, and their benefits.

Key Solar Subsidy Schemes in India

The Indian government, through the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE), has launched several schemes to promote solar energy adoption. For more details on all available schemes, visit the official MNRE website at mnre.gov.in. Here are two of the most prominent programs in 2025:

  • PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana: Launched in 2024, this scheme aims to install rooftop solar panels in 10 million homes across India, providing up to 300 units of free electricity per month. It offers subsidies of up to 40% on installation costs, depending on the system capacity (e.g., up to ₹78,000 for a 3 kW system). You can apply and learn more on the official portal at pmsuryaghar.gov.in.
  • PM-KUSUM Scheme: This scheme supports farmers by providing subsidies for solar-powered pumps and grids for agricultural use. It helps reduce dependency on diesel and grid electricity, promoting sustainable farming practices. More information is available on the MNRE website at mnre.gov.in.

Additionally, many states offer their own subsidies. For example, Gujarat provides incentives for residential solar setups, while Delhi supports solar adoption through its solar policy. Check the Delhi Solar Portal at solar.delhi.gov.in or the Uttar Pradesh New and Renewable Energy Development Agency (UPNEDA) at upneda.org.in for state-specific details.

Eligibility and Application Process

To be eligible for most solar subsidies in India, you generally need to meet the following criteria:

  • Be an Indian citizen with a valid electricity connection.
  • Own a property with a roof suitable for solar panel installation.
  • Not have availed any other solar subsidy for the same property.
  • Use MNRE-approved vendors and equipment for installation.

The application process has been simplified through the National Portal for Rooftop Solar, where you can register, apply, and track your subsidy status. Visit the portal at pmsuryaghar.gov.in to get started. You’ll need documents like ID proof, address proof, electricity bill, and proof of property ownership. Benefits of Solar Subsidies

Government subsidies for solar energy offer numerous benefits:

  • Cost Reduction: Subsidies can cover up to 40% of installation costs, making solar systems more affordable.
  • Electricity Savings: Schemes like PM Surya Ghar provide up to 300 units of free electricity monthly, reducing bills significantly.
  • Environmental Impact: Solar energy reduces reliance on fossil fuels, lowering carbon emissions and supporting India’s sustainability goals.
  • Additional Income: Through net metering, you can sell surplus power to the grid, earning credits or income.

For more insights into India’s renewable energy initiatives, explore the National Portal of India at india.gov.in.

Conclusion

Government subsidies for solar energy in India are a game-changer, making clean energy accessible and affordable for households and businesses in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, and beyond. By leveraging schemes like PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana and PM-KUSUM, you can reduce costs, save on electricity bills, and contribute to a sustainable future.
